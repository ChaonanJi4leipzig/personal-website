Chaonan Ji

Post-doctoral Researcher, Leipzig University

About

Post-doctoral Researcher at Leipzig University, Germany. My research focuses on remote sensing, climate extremes, and ecosystem science, with expertise in hyperspectral image classification, time-series analysis, and machine learning for Earth observation.

Research Interests

  • Remote sensing – hyperspectral image classification (laboratory, airborne, spaceborne), time-series analysis, multispectral–hyperspectral data integration, urban heat-island studies.
  • Climate extremes – detection of compound heat-wave and drought events; forecasting vegetation impacts.
  • Ecosystem science – assessment of vegetation condition, dynamics, resistance, resilience; plant-trait retrieval from hyperspectral data.
  • Techniques – Earth-system data-cube generation; machine-learning forecasting models.

Education

  • Postdoc, Leipzig University, Germany (2022-03 – present)
    Supervisor: Prof. Dr. Miguel D. Mahecha
    Project - TEE Cube: 2024-03 – 2025-09, as project lead.
    DLR project under CEOS call, BMWK project. Full name: Time-varying AI-based mapping of ecosystem conditions and extents using multi-source Earth observation data cubes. The project constructs Earth system data cubes and bridges multispectral-derived spectral indices with hyperspectral-derived plant traits using AI forecasting methods, to monitor ecosystem diversity and dynamics, showcasing the Bavarian Forest in Germany.
    Project - DeepExtremes: 2022-03 – 2024-06, as project manager.
    ESA project, part of the AI4SCIENCE activity. The project focuses on the detection, prediction, and explainability of machine learning methods and explainable AI for vegetation response to extreme events, multi-hazards, and composite events.
  • Ph.D. in Geography, Humboldt University of Berlin & German Aerospace Center (DLR), Germany (2018-10 – 2022-04)
    Thesis title: Mapping urban surface materials using imaging spectroscopy data.
    Supervisors: Dr. Uta Heiden, Prof. Hannes Feilhauer, Prof. Tobia Lakes
  • DAAD Visiting Student, GFZ Potsdam, Germany (2017-01 – 2017-03)
    Research topic: A fast and reliable classification approach for change detection and trend analysis with Landsat data.
    Supervisor: Dr. Nina Boesche
  • M.Sc. in Space Physics, Shandong University, China (2015-09 – 2018-06)
    Thesis title: Key issues in change detection of multi-temporal satellite images.
    Supervisors: Prof. Dr. Hermann Josef Kaufmann, Prof. Dr. Guochang Xu
  • B.Sc. in Space Science and Technology, Shandong University, China (2011-09 – 2015-06)
    Supervisor: Prof. Dr. Guochang Xu
